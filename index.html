Nanjing, Fall of 2010

Jan 25th, 2013

My time spent in Nanjing truly was a special one as it was my very first opportunity outside the USA. I was looking forward to experiencing new sights, sounds, and flavors and I had only been in the country for just a few short hours. Had it not been for the kindness of perfect strangers, it is very likely that I would have had a rough time getting to the hotel that had been previously arranged for the group of study abroad students of which I was a part.

It was raining when the van pulled up to the hotel. I did my best to graciously thank my driver and headed into the lobby of the hotel. There I was, face to face with the Chinese language. They only spoke numbers in English to help me understand amounts of money owed, etc. After paying what I could only assume was either the correct, or perhaps more than correct, amount of money for my room, I carried my luggage to my room to take a small break from the very different world I was in.

I have a hard time recollecting what took place next, except that I remember feeling nervous about navigating my way around Nanjing without a map. However, my days of playing text-based MUDs online provided me with some amount of confidence to wander about in areas very, very unfamiliar to me without getting lost. I stumbled upon a small soup kitchen and ordered what the guy siting at the table to my right had ordered.
